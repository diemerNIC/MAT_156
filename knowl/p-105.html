If we connect propositions \(p\) and \(q\) with or, then the result is called the inclusive disjunction of \(p\) and \(q\text{.}\) The inclusive disjunction is true if at least one of \(p\) and \(q\) is true. It is false only if both \(p\) and \(q\) are false. Symbolically, we write

\begin{equation*} p \myor q \end{equation*}
