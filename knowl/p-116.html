To evaluate the proposition \(\notq\myand p\text{,}\) you'd negate \(q\) first, and then form the conjunction with \(p\text{.}\) You could write \((\notq)\myand p\text{,}\) but the brackets are redundant. If you want the conjunction to occur before the negation, then you must write \(\mynot(q\myand p)\text{.}\) It is very important that you recognize that \(\notq\myand p\) is not equivalent to \(\mynot (q\myand p)\text{.}\)
