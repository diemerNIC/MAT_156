Answer 2.7.5.1.

The conditional here is
\begin{equation*} (\text{sleep or traffic}){\rightarrow}\text{ late} \end{equation*}
The contrapositive is then
\begin{align*} \sim\!{\text{late}} \amp {\rightarrow} \sim\!{\text{sleep or traffic}}\\ \amp \sim\!{\text{sleep}}\text{ and }\sim\!{\text{traffic}} \end{align*}
Therefore, the contrapositive is “If Jason is not late for class, then he didn't sleep in and he didn't get caught in traffic.” The or changes into and, as indicated by De Morgan's Law.
