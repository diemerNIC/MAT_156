1. The pattern is that you add 4 to the previous term to get the next term. The next term is then 23.
  2. The pattern is that if you say that ``1’’ is the first term and ``4’’ is the second term, then \(n^2\) will be the \(\nth{}\) term. So the next term after 36 is 49.
  3. The pattern is to divide each term by two (or multiply by one-half) to get the next term. So the term after $1/16$ will be $1/32$.
  4. The pattern is to multiply each term by $-2$ to get the next term. The next term is then 48.
  5. The pattern is to subtract 9 from the previous term, so the next one is \(-33\text{.}\)
