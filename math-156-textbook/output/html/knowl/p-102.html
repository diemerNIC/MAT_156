If we connect the propositions \(p\) and \(q\) with and, then the result is called the conjunction of \(p\) and \(q\text{.}\) The conjunction of \(p\) and \(q\) is true provided both \(p\) and \(q\) are true. Otherwise it is false. Symbolically, we write
\begin{equation*} p {\wedge} q \end{equation*}
