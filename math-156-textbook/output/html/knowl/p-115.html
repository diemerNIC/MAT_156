To evaluate the proposition \(p{\vee} q{\wedge} r\text{,}\) you would evaluate \(q{\wedge} r\) first, and then form the inclusive disjunction with \(p\) (informally "’or it’ with \(p\)"). So \(p{\vee} q{\wedge} r\) is the same as \(p{\vee}(q{\wedge} r)\text{.}\) The precedence of the connectives makes the brackets redundant, but when you are starting out it might be helpful to include them.
