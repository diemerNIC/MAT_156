To evaluate the proposition \(\sim\!q\wedge p\text{,}\) you'd negate \(q\) first, and then form the conjunction with \(p\text{.}\) You could write \((\sim\!q)\wedge p\text{,}\) but the brackets are redundant. If you want the conjunction to occur before the negation, then you must write \(\sim\!(q\wedge p)\text{.}\) It is very important that you recognize that \(\sim\!q\wedge p\) is not equivalent to \(\sim\!(q\wedge p)\text{.}\)
