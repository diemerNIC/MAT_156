In the same way, we can construct truth tables to prove the distributive laws, in both logic and Boolean algebra notation. Note carefully the forms of these laws.

Distributive Laws.

Logic
\begin{align*} p{\wedge}(q{\vee} r)\amp\Leftrightarrow(p{\wedge} q){\vee}(p{\wedge} r)\\ p{\vee}(q{\wedge} r)\amp\Leftrightarrow(p{\vee} q){\wedge}(p{\vee} r) \end{align*}
Boolean
\begin{align*} A(B+C)\amp =AB+AC\\ A+BC\amp =(A+B)(A+C) \end{align*}

The first of the distributive laws in Boolean notation looks like the usual one from arithmetic with real numbers. The second one, however, looks decidedly unlike any rules from arithmetic. You must not let the familiar rules of arithmetic distract you from the laws of logic.
