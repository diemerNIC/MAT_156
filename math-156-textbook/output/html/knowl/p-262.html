Given the conditional statement \(\conditional\text{,}\) the statement \(\inverse\) is called the inverse. Once again, let's look at our example:

Original statement: If Barney is a dog, then Barney has four legs.

Inverse: If Barney is not a dog, then Barney does not have four legs.

From this example, we see that the original statement and its inverse are not logically equivalent.
