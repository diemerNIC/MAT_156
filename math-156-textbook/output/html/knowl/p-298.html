A series is the sum of the terms of a finite or infinite sequence. Here are two examples:

  1. \(\displaystyle 16 + 20 + 24 + 28 + \ldots + 64\)

  2. \(\displaystyle 1 + \frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{9} + \frac{1}{{27}} + ...\)

The first example is a finite series, while the second one is infinite. The classification as finite or infinite is based on the number of terms being summed.
