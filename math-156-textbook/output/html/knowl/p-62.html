If, for some reason, we wish to stay in the land of integers, we could report the result by saying that 13 divided by 5 equals 2, with 3 left over. In this example, the 2 is called the quotient and the 3 is called the remainder, and we can write this calculation in the following form:
\begin{equation*} 13=\underbrace{2}_{\text{quotient}}\times 5 + \underbrace{3}_{\text{remainder}} \end{equation*}
