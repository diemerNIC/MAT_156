Solution 2.1.4.9.1.

The particular phrasing in this sentence indicates that care is being taken to avoid interpreting this as "Rich is not seven feet tall or Susan has brown hair." That statement would be represented as \(\sim\!{p}{\vee} q\) (or, more redundantly, \((\sim\!{p}){\vee} q\)).

Given the way the sentence is written, we should interpret it as \(\sim\! (p{\vee} q)\text{.}\) Later, when you study De Morgan's rules, you will learn a way to write \(\sim\!(p{\vee} q)\) in English that avoids any chance of misinterpretation.
