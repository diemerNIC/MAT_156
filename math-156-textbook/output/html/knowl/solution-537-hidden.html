The paper is initially 0.097 mm thick with no folds. After one fold, the thickness will be \(0.097\times2\text{,}\) after two folds \(0.097\times2\times2\text{,}\) etc. So our starting term (zero folds) will be \(a_0=0.097\) and then will double with r = 2 thereafter, where \(n\) is not only the index but also the number of folds made. So \({a_n} = 0.097{\left( 2 \right)^n}\text{,}\) and the term with seven folds will be \({a_7} = 0.097{\left( 2 \right)^7} = 12.416\text{,}\) so we can conclude that the paper thickness will be 12.4 mm, or just over 1 cm thick. If I remember correctly, they resorted to C-clamps and hitting the fold with a hammer to flatten it.)
